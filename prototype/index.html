How Big is the Pot?

Hover over the various bars to see the dollar value of each listed government revenue source. On the bubble chart, if you look between the brown and red bubbles, you’ll see the spec representing asset forfeiture revenue and how many orders of magnitude bigger other revenue sources are, like income tax or even customs duties. This helps us to level set on just how much money we’re talking about. While it may not be a lot in relation to all federal government revenue, the impact is has on those affected by it is much bigger.

Following the Money!

Prior to 2015, there is no data on case outcome. And even after, between 2015-2019, you can hover over the bars to see how much monetary value has no case outcome attached to it. This shows the volume of cases that are never taken to trial. Because civil asset forfeiture works on a “guilty until proven innocent” basis, a lot of times when defendants cannot afford lawyers, they just relinquish their property without a trial.
